Motivation

  • Magnetotail is a very dynamic region which is
    poorly covered by observational data
  • Magnetotail dynamics contribute to space weather hazards
    • Geomagnetically induced currents
    • Spacecraft charging
    • Spacecraft anomalies
    • Communication outages
  • Magnetotail is not always considered in forecasting of space weather

Motivation

  • MHD captures global dynamics, including
    connections between different regions and spatial scales
  • MHD models have been used to study the magnetosphere for many years
  • Few MHD validation efforts have focused on the magnetotail

Objectives

  • Extend the validation of MHD into the magnetotail
    region
  • Explore the connections between the magnetosphere and ionosphere
  • Test whether MHD can consistently reproduce observed substorms

Space Weather Modeling Framework

Solar wind inputs

Geomagnetic indices estimate magnetospheric current systems

From Stern (1994)
  • Currents are constantly changing
  • Currents produce a magnetic field that can be observed from the ground
  • Geomagnetic indices are computed from ground-based magnetic field measurements

Sym-H index estimates azimuthal current strength

From Stern (1994)
Sym-H
  • Computed from low-latitude magnetometers
  • Sensitive to azimuthal currents (ring current, partial ring current, cross-tail current, magnetopause current)

Model does well at predicting Sym-H

  • Storm-time behavior
    • Ring current strengthens
    • Sym-H turns negative
  • Good Sym-H predictions
    • Indicate model has realistic ring currents
    • Important for storm-time accuracy

Isotropic boundary consists of a sharp change in particle precipitation

Scattering mechanisms

Current sheet scattering (CSS)
  • Explains presence of IB at all times and MLTs
  • Occurs when field line curvature \(r_c\) is small and gyroradius \(r_g\) is large
  • Controlled by \(K=\frac{r_c}{r_g}\)
EMIC wave scattering
  • Requires presence of EMIC waves
  • Wave frequency must be near ion gyrofrequency
  • EMIC waves are more common during active times
Objectives
  • Constrain the range of \(K\) associated with CSS
  • Assess the relative importance of CSS during storms

Field lines traced to determine origin of IB particles

  • Trace starts at IB observation
  • \(K\) estimated where field line crosses current sheet
  • THEMIS satellites provide magnetic field observations in the vicinity

\(K\) correlates with \(\Delta B_z\)

  • Model error in \(B_z\) obtained using THEMIS observations
  • Correlation between \(K\) and \(\Delta B_z\) allows part of the error in \(K\) to be removed

Corrected \(K\)'s fall in a fairly narrow range

IB properties vary significantly during storms

Sym-H and MLT correlate with IB latitude but not \(K^*\)

  • Indicates that CSS process is active for a significant portion of IB observations

Storm-time \(K\) is often within the range for CSS

  • Between 21% to 69% of \(K^*\) values are in range for CSS, depending on threshold and latitude set used
  • Indicates that CSS is not the sole mechanism

Summary

Substorm validation effort

Can SWMF predict substorms?

  1. Use a “vanilla” SWMF global magnetosphere set-up
  2. Feed a month (January, 2005) of observed solar wind data into it
  3. Look for substorms...

...this last step is complicated by the lack of an agreed-upon method for identifying substorms

Substorm identification

  • Substorms can be identified in many ways
  • Each method produces false positives and misses
  • Using all datasets together could improve accuracy

Combining signatures

  1. Convolve each onset list with a Gaussian kernel
  2. Add convolved scores together
  3. Identify local maxima that exceed a threshold
Tuning parameters:
  • Kernel width = 10 min
  • Threshold = 2.5
  • Could be different for model and observations

Combining signatures reduces false positives and data gaps

Conclusions

  • SWMF does well in predicting geomagnetic indices
  • CSS plays dominant role for IB formation during quiet conditions and a significant role during storm conditions
  • Substorm identification procedure fulfills a community need to move toward multiple-signature identification
    • False identifications are rejected
    • Data gaps are filled
  • Substorm prediction with MHD is possible
